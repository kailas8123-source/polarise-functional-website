Our Story

Born of ice. Belonging to all.

Polarise began as a small field fund for polar bear monitoring and grew into a public conservation platform linking habitat protection, education, and direct action.

Why Polarise Exists

The Arctic is not remote to the life of the planet. Sea ice cools global systems, supports Indigenous knowledge and livelihoods, and gives polar bears the hunting platform they need to raise the next generation.

Our work is designed around practical conservation: protect denning areas, reduce preventable conflict, support research teams, and help people turn concern into repeatable action.

Impact Timeline

2019 - Field Listening

Partnered with Arctic guides and researchers to identify denning corridors and seasonal risk points.

2022 - Community Tools

Launched education kits for schools, visitor centers, and volunteer teams.

2026 - Polarise Network

Expanded into a donor-supported platform for habitat grants, public data, and action events.
